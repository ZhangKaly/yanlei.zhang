I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Mila – Quebec AI Institute, working with Guy Wolf and Yoshua Bengio. I also collaborate closely with Smita Krishnaswamy's lab at Yale University, and I am affiliated with the AIDOS group under Bastian Rieck at Helmholtz Munich.

I received my Ph.D. in Mathematics from Queen's University under the supervision of Professor Andrew Lewis.

My research focuses on developing novel machine learning methodologies informed by tools from advanced mathematics, with a focus on applications in biomedical discovery. Current interests include:

  • Optimal transport, generative modeling, and deep learning to explore cellular development and trajectory analysis
  • Diffusion geometry and manifold learning for high-dimensional and single-cell data
  • Graph signal processing and PDEs for molecular structure prediction
  • Neural representation learning and brain activity investigation
