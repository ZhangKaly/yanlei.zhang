Queen's University

I am an Assistant Professor at School of Computing at Queen’s University.

Previously, I was a postdoc working with groups of Guy Wolf, Yoshua Bengio and Eugene Belilovsky (Mila – Quebec AI Institute), Smita Krishnaswamy (Yale University), and Bastian Rieck (Helmholtz Munich).

My research focuses on developing novel machine learning methodologies informed by tools from advanced mathematics, with a focus on applications in biomedical discovery. My current interests include:

  • Optimal transport, generative modeling, and deep learning to explore cellular development and trajectory analysis
  • Diffusion geometry and manifold learning for high-dimensional and single-cell data
  • Graph signal processing and PDEs for molecular structure prediction
  • Neural representation learning and brain activity investigation
